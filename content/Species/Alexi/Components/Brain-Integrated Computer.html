Brain-Integrated Computer

Installation
...

Although treated as one complex computer, a BIC consists of several chips mounted on and in the brain, directly connected to certain areas of it. An Alexi cannot function without it.

The BIC connects via wires to the visor and neck ports, hidden inside the head.

Standard Connection Ports
...

The ports are installed directly on the HBNC and consist of at least one Standard Connection Port. More ports may be installed (auxiliary ports) but usually only one is needed. The SCP protocol is backwards compatible with USB, although it does not support the high-bandwidth and Alexi-specific optimizations.

Connection requires proper authorization or consent from the Alexi that is being connected to. Most system settings can be accessed externally this way. Alexi-to-Alexi P2P connections are also supported, allowing private conversation and data transmission over cable.

Function
...

The standard BIC, when first installed on an Alexi’s brain, stimulates and trains the brain to know how to walk, talk, read and write, as well as other functions that are needed for their specific purpose.

Those basic skills and behaviors are integrated into the Alexi in a similar manner to VR training, or “forced dreams”, where the Alexi lives out pre-programmed scenarios in their head and actively learns from them. This process is extremely quick before fully activating an Alexi, but “dream-injecting” becomes impossible after the creature becomes awake and aware for the first time.

VR training material is usually artificially crafted, but sometimes real memory recordings are used. (Check subsection below)

Full-Memory Cloning
...

The reverse may also be done; an Alexi’s memories can be downloaded in full, or partially, into another VR training medium used to train another fresh Alexi. This method can help with creating an Alexi’s “backup”.

If an entire life’s worth of memories trains an Alexi, they effectively become them. So, in case of real death, the Alexi’s mind can be “restored” into a new one, as it is not hard to create a genetically identical clone to upload them into. Though beware that full-memory cloning can cause early corruption.

Later down the line Project Prisma was invented, which is believed to more accurately transfer consciousness.

Controversy
...

Please note that using the method described above to “resurrect” a dead Alexi is highly effective and creates a copy of the person with identical personality, wants, needs and likes, they theoretically are not the same Alexi, as they are physically different on the smaller scale.

There is no 100% perfect cloning, so there will be extremely small, more or less noticeable differences (such as being made from completely different atoms, and grown all over again in a lab).

Alexis made by this method may also be argued to not be the same on the spiritual level (which is not proven to either exist nor not exist) as physically, they are someone completely different with implanted memories. Thinking about this too much might instill an existential crisis, but please beware of these points.
