The planet type consists of its class and category.

Classes
...

Class A/A+
...

This class of planets indicates modern, up-to-date technologies which are widely available there all over the world. The A+ status is granted if the planet is also a hub for research of further technologies.

Class B/B+
...

Indicates up-to-date technology, but which is only available to the planet's richest inhabitants. The B+ status is granted if the planet is majorly contributing to research.

Class C
...

This class shows a substantial lag behind modern technologies, often caused by the lack of resources to construct them.

Class D
...

Indicates total lack of modern technology, but still requires a central government. These are usually freshly-colonized worlds on their way to become class B or higher, or worlds which have been abandoned.

Class E
...

This class is reserved for planets which have no greater social structure and/or completely lack technology. These usually consist of fresh Splitworlds.

Categories
...

Stableworld
...

Stableworlds are what most people call "home". These planets have a centralized faction government and are usually Class B or higher.

Graveworld
...

Now desolate planets. Destroyed and abandoned after fights between factions occurred. Barren wastelands, destroyed cities and no civilization presence. Often used as testing grounds for new technologies, weapons or other, more commercial use. Most can be found in the Outlands.

Splitworld
...

Independent planets which house several independent nations without a central government. Those usually form in Uncharted Horizons, where no faction has claimed territory.

Rogueworld
...

Adjacent to Splitworlds, although Rogueworlds have a centralized government, the planet is not part of the faction which claimed the sector. They range from Class B to D, and political tensions are usually high, as factions owning the sector they are housed in usually do not like when part of their territory is not under their control.

Techworld
...

Contrary to their name, Techworlds are not highly-classed planets, but instead planets used with the sole purpose of extracting resources. The environment on them inhospitable and sometimes heavily toxic.

Wildworld
...

A planet mostly overtaken by vegetation and non-sapient life. For a world to be considered a Wildworld, it must be a class E planet without any major outside influence.
