Head-Body Neck Connector

The head can be easily separated from the body without complications with the use of a HBNC, meaning an Alexi can easily get a replacement. Alexis were genetically engineered to be as interchangeable as possible, and the species accepts artificial implants extremely well, and can even have organs transplanted without the use of immunosuppressants.

Alexis can turn their heads about 100 degrees to the left and to the right, with a total 200 degree motion. This stop is artificial, but prevents the Alexi from accidentally disconnecting themselves.

Usage and operation
...

To disconnect an Alexi, simply:
...

  1. Place your hands in the following order: one on the visor, one on the back of the head
  2. Slowly turn the head either to the left or right.
    1. Do not hesitate. You will not damage your Alexi.
  3. Continue turning past the normal 100-degree range of motion of your Alexi. This should not be uncomfortable.
  4. At 180 degrees (half-turn), the visor should turn off and the head will hibernate.
  5. After you hear a click, it is safe to lift up the head off the neck.
    1. You should not feel any resistance. If you do, you might damage your Alexi. If you do, slightly push the head down into the slot and consult the reconnection guide.

Reconnection guide
...

  1. Place the head on the neck connector, facing a half-turn from the normal position.
  2. Push down until you hear a click
  3. Rotate the head 180 degrees until it's in the normal position
  4. The head should initiate shortly.
    1. If any errors occur, watch the error code displayed on the visor, and act in accordance to the manual.
    2. If the visor does not turn on to display an error code, initiate a connection via the Standard Connection Port and consult the manual.

Disconnects
...

When detached, an Alexi head will always go into hibernation as a safety precaution to insure survival.

A detached Alexi head can survive on its own for approximately 36 to 48 hours in sleep mode. They cannot be conscious in this state in order to preserve oxygen and energy. Cryogenically frozen heads can survive indefinitely.

Connector Issues / Paralysis
...

Sometimes, the HBNC can malfunction due to external damage to the neck or improper installation. Most of the time, if the HBNC gets damaged, it cannot get easily fixed and cannot be outright replaced. A broken HBNC is a death sentence for an Alexi.

However, there are cases where irreparable damage to the connector is not lethal. Alexis can become paralyzed from the neck down due to improper nerve alignment or outright failure to connect. Their bodies and/or LTSUs can still supply the head with all needed nutrients and oxygen.
