TLDR Core Features (aka. how you can make one for yourself as an OC!)
Pictured: Alexi head

Description
...

An Alexi is an animal head with a Visor as its face and a brain-integrated computer (BIC). The head extends a bit into the neck, and cuts off at a head-body neck connector point, a HBNC.

These Alexi heads are not self-sufficient and must be connected either to an Alexi body or a Long-Term Sustenance Unit. They may also be cryogenically frozen for longer storage.

Speaking of bodies, they come in all shapes and sizes. They can be fully artificial or partly biological, but due to the connector requirement and a few extra organs, they cannot be fully biological on the inside, although may appear so from the outside. The head must always be biological w/ visor.

Origin
...

The first ever Alexi, model AA-00000-Proto was made after countless attempts by The Intergalactic Federation in creating the perfect, humane slave force. But with androids, they could just never get the emotional intelligence they hoped for from a machine. No matter how strong of a computer they fit inside an artificial head, the clean-thinking, emotionless algorithms were never quite up to the standard of a being that people would like to interact with. Countless hours of research amounted to over 60% genetically artificial creatures, with the rest being infused from other species in order to create a specific appearance.

These creatures were extremely expensive to make, and did not have long lifespans. The Federation needed something cheap and readily replaceable in the workforce, so one day, they finally came up with the idea to separate the expensive to manufacture, but easy to transport head with the organic brain from the easily replaceable body, and thus the current Alexis came to be.

Physiological Needs
...

Sleep
...

An Alexi must sleep, just like every organic brain, although this process is heavily aided by the BIC, speeding up the process. An Alexi of AA ranking only needs about 2 hours of sleep per night, increasing with complexity. Model XX Alexis need about 4 to 5 hours. Any Alexi may skip sleep and pull all-nighters until exhaustion. It is not healthy and is not advised in fear of accelerating corruption, but a full night’s sleep (8 to 10 hours) after a few days of staying up will reset their biological clocks.

Nutrition
...

They also need nutrients and water to survive. All of their biological components need calories, protein, fats, vitamins, etc. to function properly. The calorie intake requirement of an Alexi is negligible because of the Calorie Electric Energy Converter (if they recharge via other means), and the on-board battery can last up to 60 days without any caloric intake, and recharges itself when the Alexi consumes food. Keep in mind that nutrients and water still need to be ingested. If an Alexi gains all of their energy through electricity, they will suffer malnutrition.

Waste Disposal
...

Alexis produce no solid waste. The nanite-aided GI tract in their body strips everything of the last available resource before releasing it as odorless gas out the rear end. They have a functional urinary tract aided with nanite filters with its sole purpose to regulate osmosis (electrolyte-water balance) and do not dispose of waste using it. Basically, only (sometimes salty) water comes out of the urethra and it is completely transparent, sterile H20 with some impurities. Although socially questionable, it is completely safe and reasonable to drink this water.

Safety Mechanisms
...

Any trauma sustained “neck-down” is mostly safe to the Alexi head. A lot of research went into death prevention. It is always easier and cheaper to replace the body only.

Bleeding
...

Low blood pressure is automatically detected by the system, and if it goes low enough, the HBNC will sever the connection to the body in order to prevent damage to the brain and hibernate the Alexi. It is important to know that this function cannot stop the source of the bleeding.

Starvation / Dehydration
...

Just like your phone, the battery on an Alexi will never truly reach 0%. They shut down long before that in order to save themselves. In the case of dehydration, all the water remaining in their body will be redirected into their brain in order to keep optimal levels. If it gets too low for the body, the Alexi shuts down.

Failsafes and Recovery
...

When an Alexi body is not in use, the secondary body computer will stop most natural processes within it. However it is not as efficient as head hibernation.

Alexi hearts can be freely started and stopped without repercussions with the use of an electronic component. This component can sometimes fail. The system detects that and will not allow the Alexi to start.

Any other organ is also closely monitored by the system. Failures and warnings are known to the Alexi and they can notify their owners and/or keepers if they need repairs

Life Expectancy
...

The life expectancy of an Alexi hugely varies by the model. Usually, the more expensive the Alexi, the longer it lives, but that is not always the case. There is no theoretical limit in their bodies, as they can simply get a replacement if they act soon enough. Most likely natural causes of death for Alexis include:

  • Brain cancer
  • Corruption (Unknown if it’s truly natural)
  • Wear on the HBNC or BIC (although minor damage is auto-repaired by the nanites, if the Alexi head is mishandled and improperly stored, natural corrosive processes can occur)

Most common-use Alexis live for at least 50 years without experiencing symptoms of any of the above, although the body might wear out a lot faster (or slower) depending on the quality of materials used (both genetic and artificial).
